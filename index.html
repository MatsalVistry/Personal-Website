Qualifications and Skills

A majority of my skillset revolves around backend development. Solving logic problems, making optimizations, and finding clever solutions to complex tasks are some of the core responsibilities of backend development and align with my interests.

Although I intend to become a software engineer, I initially wanted to pursue a career in data science. The idea of developing models to perform computations that aren't humanly possible fascinates me. That said, during a past data science internship, I found myself leaning towards development rather than analysis. Rather than tuning and constant iteration, I found myself enjoying the breadth of software engineering rather than pocketing myself into a niche field.

The constantly changing technologies are one of my favorite parts of software engineering. In the past two years of being exposed to the industry, I have learned and enjoyed the experience of working with backend technologies, databases, cloud computing, and general tools.

I have learned a lot through my internship experiences, with one of the biggest things being communication. Not making assumptions and asking lots of questions can prevent future issues and save time from refactoring. As an engineer, it's natural to make a barebones plan and begin writing code for it, but this has almost always led to refactoring from my experience. I also find communication to be very important when working in multidisciplinary teams since people always use technical language that is niche to their field. This becomes exponentially worse the longer the clarifications are unresolved. In my opinion, a good developer doesn't only write good code, but also communicates clearly about his current work and future intentions.

My future goals are to become a solutions architect. I can say this with confidence as it aligns with my interest in solving complex problems and staying up to date with modern technologies. As for short term goals, I want to involve myself with cloud technologies and distributed computing.
